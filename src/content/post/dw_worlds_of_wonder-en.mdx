This day was really fun.

The original plan was just to visit the San Diego Museum of Art. The sky was a little gray, the ground was still wet, and everything felt quiet. Then while passing the Comic-Con Museum, there was a glimpse of some very familiar blue boxes, with `Doctor Who Worlds of Wonder` across them.

There had been absolutely no idea this exhibition was here. It was not the reason for the trip, and it was not something checked in advance either. It just appeared out of nowhere while walking by.

<img src="https://cdn.lyt0112.com/Posts/dw_worlds_of_wonder/IMG_2393.jpeg" alt="Comic-Con Museum at Balboa Park" style="width: 100%; height: auto; display: block; margin: 0 auto;" />
<p style="text-align: center; font-style: italic; color: gray;">The Doctor Who banner outside Comic-Con Museum</p>

Getting closer, it turned out to be real.

<img src="https://cdn.lyt0112.com/Posts/dw_worlds_of_wonder/IMG_2394.jpeg" alt="Doctor Who Worlds of Wonder flyer" style="width: 70%; height: auto; display: block; margin: 0 auto;" />
<p style="text-align: center; font-style: italic; color: gray;">Exhibition flyer</p>

Tickets were about $30 for adults and $20 for students. At the time, signing up on the official site also got a $15 discounted ticket.

## That Blue Door

The entrance itself was simple, but once that blue door showed up, the whole place already felt very Doctor Who.

<img src="https://cdn.lyt0112.com/Posts/dw_worlds_of_wonder/IMG_2398.jpeg" alt="Exhibition entrance shaped like the TARDIS" style="width: 100%; height: auto; display: block; margin: 0 auto;" />
<p style="text-align: center; font-style: italic; color: gray;">Exhibition entrance</p>

The first thing there was the costume wall.

<img src="https://cdn.lyt0112.com/Posts/dw_worlds_of_wonder/IMG_2400.jpeg" alt="Doctor costumes from modern eras" style="width: 100%; height: auto; display: block; margin: 0 auto;" />
<p style="text-align: center; font-style: italic; color: gray;">Modern Doctor costumes</p>

Nine, Ten, Eleven, Twelve, Thirteen. For anyone who has been following the show for years, it still feels special. Twelve especially brought back 2014, when Doctor Who first became a real part of everyday life.

Further in were the console and the sonic screwdrivers. Even got to touch the console once.

<img src="https://cdn.lyt0112.com/Posts/dw_worlds_of_wonder/IMG_2406.jpeg" alt="TARDIS console" style="width: 100%; height: auto; display: block; margin: 0 auto;" />

In the nearby case were some of the smaller props that do not show up as often, including Twelve's sonic shades and the little device used to scan Zygon Queen Elizabeth in the 2013 50th Anniversary Special.

<img src="https://cdn.lyt0112.com/Posts/dw_worlds_of_wonder/IMG_2410.jpeg" alt="Props and gadgets from Doctor Who" style="width: 80%; height: auto; display: block; margin: 0 auto;" />

After the outfit area, the first thing to see was a row of three spacesuits. The one on the left is River Song's Apollo-style suit from the `S06` story arc. The middle one is from the library two-parter, `S04E08-S04E09`, and it immediately brings back `Hey, who turned out the lights?`. The orange one on the right is that classic Doctor Who spacesuit that appears again and again, including Clara's in `S08E07`.

<img src="https://cdn.lyt0112.com/Posts/dw_worlds_of_wonder/IMG_2416.jpeg" alt="Space suits in the exhibition" style="width: 100%; height: auto; display: block; margin: 0 auto;" />
